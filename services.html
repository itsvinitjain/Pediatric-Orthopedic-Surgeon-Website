Services Provided

Paediatric Trauma

Pediatric trauma refers to injuries sustained by children, typically ranging from infancy to adolescence. Managing pediatric trauma requires specialized medical knowledge and a unique approach due to the anatomical and physiological differences between children and adults.

CTEV

Congenital Talipes Equinovarus (CTEV), commonly known as clubfoot, is a congenital condition characterized by abnormal foot positioning, where the foot turns inward and downward.

Cerebral Palsy

Cerebral Palsy (CP) is a group of permanent movement and posture disorders that stem from non-progressive brain damage occurring during early development. This condition affects muscle control, coordination, and sometimes cognitive function.

Hip Preservation Surgery

Hip preservation surgery refers to a set of surgical techniques aimed at treating various conditions that affect the hip joint, typically in younger individuals, to avoid or delay the need for a total hip replacement. It encompasses procedures like hip arthroscopy, periacetabular osteotomy, and femoroacetabular osteoplasty.

Limb Deformities

Limb deformities refer to abnormal or irregular shapes, lengths, or orientations of limbs, typically caused by various factors such as congenital conditions, trauma, infections, or growth abnormalities. These deformities can affect bones, joints, muscles, and other tissues, leading to functional impairments.

Foot Deformities

Foot deformities encompass various abnormalities in foot structure, including conditions like clubfoot, flat feet, bunions, and hammertoes. These deformities can impact mobility, cause discomfort, and affect footwear choices.

Surgery

Surgery involves medical procedures that typically require incisions to treat or correct various conditions, injuries, or diseases. Surgical interventions can be elective or necessary, aiming to restore function, alleviate pain, remove tumors, repair injuries, or address deformities.

Osteomyelitis

Osteomyelitis is a serious infection of the bone, often caused by bacteria. It can result from direct contamination, bloodstream infections, or nearby soft tissue infections. Symptoms include pain, swelling, redness, and fever.

Benign Tumors

A benign tumor is an abnormal growth of cells that lacks the ability to invade nearby tissues or spread to distant parts of the body (metastasize). While non-cancerous, benign tumors can still cause problems if they grow large or affect surrounding structures.

Deformity Correction

Deformity correction refers to medical procedures aimed at correcting abnormal shapes or alignments of bones, joints, or tissues in the body. This can include limb lengthening, spinal curvature correction, and joint realignment.

Congenital Anomalies

Congenital anomalies, also known as birth defects or congenital disorders, are structural or functional abnormalities present at birth. These anomalies can affect various body parts and systems, resulting from genetic, environmental, or unknown factors during prenatal development. They may range from mild and manageable conditions to severe and life-threatening ones.

First Aid

First aid refers to the initial assistance and care provided to an injured or ill person before professional medical help arrives. It includes basic life-saving measures aimed at stabilizing the person's condition and preventing further harm.
